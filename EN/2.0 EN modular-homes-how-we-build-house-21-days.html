What distinguishes modular, prefab, and manufactured homes

The terminology around factory-built housing causes persistent confusion. Understanding the distinctions matters because they affect building code classification, financing options, and long-term value.

Modular homes are complete three-dimensional volumetric units manufactured in factories at 90-95% completion, including structure, mechanical systems, electrical wiring, plumbing, insulation, and interior finishes. They comply with the same regional building codes as site-built homes—the German Landesbauordnungen, EU averagen OIB Guidelines, or local Romanian standards. Permanently installed on concrete foundations, modular homes are classified as real property and appreciate identically to traditional construction.

Prefabricated (prefab) homes is the umbrella term covering all factory-built housing, including modular. In Germany, these are known as Fertighaus and now represent over 23% of new residential construction—rising to 30%+ for single-family homes. The term encompasses both volumetric modules and panelized systems.

Panelized construction delivers flat 2D wall, floor, and roof panels to sites for assembly—achieving only 30-50% factory completion versus modular's 90-95%. While offering greater design flexibility for complex architectural forms, panelized systems require 1-3 weeks of on-site work just for weather-tight enclosure compared to modular's single-day module setting.

Key Distinction

European modular homes must never be confused with US-style manufactured homes (built on permanent steel chassis). They share virtually nothing except partial factory production. Modular homes comply with full building codes and are permanently affixed to foundations as real property.

The factory production process

Modern modular factories operate more like automotive manufacturing plants than traditional construction sites. Computer-controlled precision, specialized workstations, and rigorous quality checkpoints transform home building from unpredictable craft into repeatable industrial process.

The European market context

Germany leads in scale

The German prefab market reached USD 7.64 billion in 2025, projecting to USD 10.23 billion by 2030. Timber dominates with 61% market share. Over 23% of German residential construction uses prefab methods (30%+ for single-family homes). Berlin mandates 30% prefab allocation for new municipal housing.

EU average excels in timber innovation

The EU leads European mass timber construction innovation. The CO2 Bonus program offers €1 per kilogram of wood used, covering up to 50% of costs. EU average supplies 20.4% of Germany's prefab wooden house imports.

Romania offers manufacturing advantages

Romania's modular market achieved 14.3% annual growth from 2020-2023, reaching €480 million. The country offers labor cost advantages with full EU compliance. Romania holds 10.65% of construction employment—the highest in the EU.

Transportation and site logistics

European module dimension limits

Standard modules measure approximately 5.3m wide × 14.5m long × 3.8m high—exceeding EU road limits. Special Transport Permits are required for every delivery. Escort vehicles are mandatory above 3.5m width, with police escorts for the largest loads. Transport is restricted to 06:00-22:00 on weekdays.

Crane requirements and site preparation

Standard modules require 100-ton crane capacity. Site staging needs minimum 8 × 8 meters of level ground. Power line clearance of 15 feet minimum is mandatory. Crane services cost €3,000-10,000.

Foundation options

Modular homes cannot use slab-on-grade because MEP connections require space beneath. Options include crawl space (2-3 weeks), full basement (3-4+ weeks), or screw piles (1-2 days with minimal excavation).

Addressing persistent misconceptions

"Modular homes are temporary structures"

Fact: Modular homes achieve 50-100+ year lifespans—identical to traditional construction. The UK's BOPAS certification validates 60-year minimum durability. They are classified as real property and subject to the same building codes.

"Modular homes all look the same"

Fact: Modern modular enables complete architectural customization. Award-winning examples include Ecospace Studios (RIBA Award winner) and HUF HAUS's premium designs. BIOBUILDS offers four distinct models from 24m² to 142m², each fully customizable.

"Resale value is lower"

Fact: Urban Institute research confirms modular housing appreciates at "essentially the same rate as site-built homes." Energy efficiency features can command premium valuations.

"Financing isn't available"

Fact: Modular homes qualify for all standard mortgage products. Germany's KfW offers favorable loans for efficient homes. EU average provides CO2 Bonus subsidies. Standard insurance covers modular homes like any permanent structure.
